Purpose and Highlights

For the last two and a half years, my goal has been to make student and teacher experience of mathematics exponentially greater through the use of digital interactives. I challenge myself and my colleagues to make curriculum that is accessible to students of all cultures, backgrounds, and abilities through my work on the Access and Equity committee and my innovations in the creation of accessible digital material. I consistently support and nurture growth in my colleagues by providing consistent feedback and offering guidance whenever needed.

I demonstrate the value of advocacy. I take pride in communicating with colleagues in a clear and timely manner. I ask clarifying questions to gather more information about teammates' perspectives, and use that information to make informed decisions. I value the diverse backgrounds and experiences of my teammates, and seek to incorporate those experiences in my decision making wherever possible.

I demonstrate the value of ambition. I am taking on new roles, such as UAT lead while continuing to lead in the PK-5 grade bands. I am discovering new ways to make digital interactives more accessible, such as using haptic feedback, sonification, and keyboard-accessible tools. I am ask for support from my colleagues and managers in order to create interactives and lessons that make the our Eureka Math products top-quality digital curriculum.

I demonstrate the value of curiosity. When making decisions with my colleagues, I seek and support multiple perspectives by ensuring that the decisions we make are positive decisions across all grade levels. I implement decisions quickly and effectively, and support others by creating methods to make their work more efficient as well. I frequently cheer on my colleagues by celebrating their successes and supporting them in sharing out their learning.

I demonstrate the value of challenge. I research advances in educational technology for elementary students and share that research out with my teammates. I share out information I learn to make sure that all of our accessibility decisions not only make our products accessible, but they also make them usable and enjoyable for students and teachers. I gather product feedback from grade level teams, DID team members, sales, students, and teachers and implement changes based on that information. I adapt to changes in roles and duties as our digital platform and projects evolve.

I demonstrate the value of enlightenment. I have been learning about accessible web design for the past year for personal growth, and I have enjoyed putting the skills I learn into practice in new and interesting ways in my professional work. I used my web development skills to devise new ways to use the components available in the platform in order to make an engaging and heretofore unexplored type of lesson for Integrated Math 1 students. I also used DOM manipulation to accomplish accessibility criteria by allowing for aria-label creation, assignment of role, canvas protection, and identification of focused elements. I have collaborated with my colleagues to implement those changes into code that makes our products stronger and more accessible.
