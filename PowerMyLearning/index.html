Play Game

Optional: Warm-Up Activity

See More

Ask [Student] if they want to review common denominators. Warm up with a short activity before playing the Learning Game!

What to Do

  1. Get two identically sized pieces of paper. They can be post-it notes, notebook paper, or scrap paper.
  2. Have the student fold one piece of paper in half. Shade one of the halves. Write the fraction ½.
  3. Have the student fold the other piece of paper in thirds. Shade one of the thirds. Write the fraction 1/3.
  4. Have the student overlap the two pieces of paper so that the fold is vertical on one and horizontal on the other.
  5. Have the student refold the pages together along the lines they already made.
  6. Have the student determine the fraction of shaded sections out of the total number of sections for each piece of paper. Have them compare that new fraction to their original fraction. 3/6=1/2 and 2/6 = 1/3.

How to Play the Game

Materials and Preparation

Overview

Play a game to add by practicing fraction comparison!

  • Create 21 cards with fractions less than 1 whose denominators are numbers up to 10. Make sure that one of the cards is the fraction ½.
  • Put the ½ card in the middle of the table.
  • Shuffle the cards and give each person 10 cards
  • Take turns placing in numerical order compared to the value of other cards.
  • Work together to ensure that the card is in the correct place. You can use models or technology to help determine that the cards are in order.
  • The game is won when all of the cards are correctly placed.
Before You Start

Materials

  • A pencil and paper
  • Number cards from 1-10

Preparation

  • Pull two cards from the pile.
  • Write the smaller number as the numerator and the larger number as the denominator of a fraction on a small piece of paper.
  • Repeat until you have 21 small pieces of paper with distinct fractions on them.
Make it Easier or Harder

Make Game Easier

  • Help your child count using your fingers or the dots on the die.
  • Write out all doubles and double +1 facts for your child to look at.
  • Try to make 3 in a row instead of 4.

Make Game More Challenging

  • Help your child count using your fingers or the dots on the die.
  • Write out all doubles and double +1 facts for your child to look at.
  • Try to make 3 in a row instead of 4.
